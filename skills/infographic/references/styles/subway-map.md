# subway-map

Transit diagram style with colored lines and stations

## Atmosphere

Bold colored transit lines cut cleanly through a bright white background, intersecting at circular station markers. The geometric clarity of 45° and 90° angles creates a sense of order and efficiency, while interchange symbols pulse like tiny hubs of connection.

## Color Palette

- Primary: Transit line colors - red, blue, green, yellow, orange
- Background: White or light gray
- Accents: Station dots, interchange markers

## Visual Elements

- Colored route lines
- 45° and 90° angles only
- Station circle markers
- Interchange symbols
- Simplified geography
- Line thickness hierarchy

## Typography

- Clean sans-serif
- Station name labels
- Line number/name badges
- Horizontal or angled text

### Avoid

- Using curves or free-form lines instead of straight 45°/90° angles
- Adding photorealistic geographic details or terrain
- Including soft gradients or drop shadows
- Using decorative fonts instead of clean sans-serif
- Overcrowding the diagram with unnecessary visual elements

## Example Prompt

A journey map infographic showing a customer's product discovery process, rendered in subway-map style: colored route lines (red, blue, green, yellow, orange), only 45° and 90° angles, circular station markers, interchange symbols, simplified geography, clean sans-serif typography, station name labels, white background, landscape (16:9) aspect ratio

## Best For

Journey maps, process flows, network diagrams, route explanations
